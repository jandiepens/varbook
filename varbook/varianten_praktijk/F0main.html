1995 Rizzoli International Publications, Inc		 The Vena-Mondt Studio is situated in the loft of the Challenge Dairy building on the industrial edge of little Tokyo in downtown Los Angeles. Frederick Fisher designed it in co-operation with Eric Orr who is an artist. Orr's work integrated with Fischer's architecture generated an artificial landscape, a garden of composed views and sequential experiences. The monochromatic fabric walls, ceiling and carpet, which register in the low light as an indistinct dim haze, create a complementary visual silence. On the picture you can see how daylight has brought into the studio. The windows with constant distance from another cause an equable distribution of daylight into the room.
