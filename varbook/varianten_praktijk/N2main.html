The Isham House consists of several different buildings around a courtyard. When entering the main house, one walks through a tall narrow hall. The ceiling of this hall smoothly changes over in the tentlike ceiling of the living room. The example on the right, showing the interior of the living room, is not exactly the same as variant N2. The roof has a slope and daylight enters the room slantwise. Still, the likeness with the simulation of variant N2 is enormous. Because of the distortion of the variant in this example the distribution of daylight in the room is even more uniform than in the variant itself.

 

The Visby General Hospital, Visby, Gotland, Sweden, 1995
ETV Arkitektkontor ABInternational Architecture Yearbook
Images Australia

 

This hospital has an area of 29,000 square metres. The structure is made of in situ concrete. Other materials that has been used are brick, steel cladding and sheet metal roofing. On the photograph on the right the interior of the entrance hall can be seen. The facade on the right seems to look like an outside facade. The treatment of daylight strengths this feeling. A window in the roof against the facade makes sure direct daylight enters the building and lightens up the facade from above just like it would look like on the outside. Reflection from direct sunlight in the windows provides for light-spots on the floor and on the back-wall.
